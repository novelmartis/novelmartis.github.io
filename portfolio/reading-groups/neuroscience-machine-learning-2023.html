Reading Group: Neuroscience x Machine Learning

Reading group course (2023) focused on key papers at the neuroscience-machine-learning interface.

Comparison of deep neural networks to spatiotemporal cortical dynamics of human visual object recognition (Cichy et al., 2016)

One of the first large-scale mapping studies showing hierarchical correspondences between deep-network layers and human visual processing in both space and time.

Paper

Recurrence is required to capture the representational dynamics of the human visual system (Kietzmann et al., 2019)

A result showing that recurrent architectures better capture time-resolved ventral-stream dynamics than purely feedforward models.

Paper

Emergence of foveal image sampling from learning to attend in visual scenes (Cheung et al., 2017)

A task-optimized attention model that learns retinal-like eccentricity-dependent sampling without imposing it by hand.

Paper

Neural scene representation and rendering (Eslami et al., 2018)

The Generative Query Network paper, showing how agents can infer compact latent scene representations and render novel viewpoints.

Paper

Recurrent computations for visual pattern completion (Tang et al., 2018)

A paper linking delayed recognition under occlusion and masking to recurrent computation in both brains and models.

Paper

Do vision transformers see like convolutional neural networks? (Raghu et al., 2021)

A comparison of representation geometry in vision transformers and convolutional networks.

Paper

A simple neural network module for relational reasoning (Santoro et al., 2017)

A demonstration that explicit relation modules can sharply improve relational reasoning in otherwise strong convolutional architectures.

Paper

Alleviating catastrophic forgetting using context-dependent gating and synaptic stabilization (Masse et al., 2018)

A straightforward continual-learning recipe combining synaptic stabilization with context-dependent sparse gating.

Paper

A hierarchy of linguistic predictions during natural language comprehension (Heilbron et al., 2022)

GPT-2-derived prediction measures combined with neural data to show concurrent multi-level predictive processing in language comprehension.

Paper

Vector-based navigation using grid-like representations in artificial agents (Banino et al., 2018)

Goal-directed agents developing grid-like codes that support vector navigation and shortcuts.

Paper

Toward goal-driven neural network models for the rodent whisker-trigeminal system (Zhuang et al., 2017)

A goal-driven modeling extension beyond primate vision into rodent somatosensation.

Paper

Single cortical neurons as deep artificial neural networks (Beniaguev et al., 2021)

A paper showing that single-neuron input-output mappings can require deep temporal architectures.

Paper

Could a neuroscientist understand a microprocessor? (Jonas and Kording, 2017)

A methodological stress test showing that standard neuroscience analyses can recover structure without true mechanistic understanding.

Paper

Reassessing hierarchical correspondences between brain and deep networks through direct interface (Sexton et al., 2022)

A re-evaluation of hierarchical brain-network correspondence using a direct interfacing framework.

Paper

Distributed representations, simple recurrent networks, and grammatical structure (Elman, 1991)

A classic paper showing how recurrent dynamics can capture grammatical regularities from sequential input.
