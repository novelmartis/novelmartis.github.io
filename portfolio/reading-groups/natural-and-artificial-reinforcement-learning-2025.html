Reading Group on Natural and Artificial Reinforcement Learning

Reading group course (2025) where each session centered on a seminal paper in reinforcement learning, spanning animal learning, cognitive neuroscience, and modern machine learning.

Pavlovian conditioning: It’s not what you think it is (Rescorla, 1988)

Associative learning foundations: conditioning goes beyond simple stimulus-response pairings and depends on richer statistical and structural relationships in learning.

Paper

Simple statistical gradient-following algorithms for connectionist reinforcement learning (Williams, 1992)

Policy-gradient foundations: REINFORCE introduced unbiased gradient estimators for policy optimization and established a core model-free paradigm still used today.

Paper

Temporal difference learning and TD-Gammon (Tesauro, 1995)

An early value-learning milestone showing that self-play and temporal-difference updates could reach expert-level behavior in complex games.

Paper

Making working memory work: A computational model of learning in the prefrontal cortex and basal ganglia (O’Reilly and Frank, 2006)

A biologically grounded actor-critic account of working-memory gating and control, bridging cortico-basal-ganglia loops with computational reinforcement learning.

Paper

Action understanding as inverse planning (Baker, Saxe, and Tenenbaum, 2009)

A Bayesian inverse-planning view of social cognition that infers latent goals from observed actions.

Paper

Human-level control through deep reinforcement learning (Mnih et al., 2015)

The DQN result that fused deep neural networks with Q-learning, replay, and target stabilization to learn Atari control directly from pixels.

Paper

The Option-Critic Architecture (Bacon, Harb, and Precup, 2017)

A hierarchical reinforcement-learning framework that learns options, intra-option policies, and termination end to end.

Paper

Prioritized memory access explains planning and hippocampal replay (Mattar and Daw, 2018)

A normative replay-prioritization rule connecting model-based planning utility to forward and backward replay patterns observed in biology.

Paper

Mastering Atari with discrete world models (Hafner et al., 2021)

DreamerV2 showed how a learned world model and imagined trajectories can support strong Atari performance with latent-space planning.

Paper

The learning of prospective and retrospective cognitive maps within neural circuits (Namboodiri and Stuber, 2021)

A perspective on prospective and retrospective reinforcement learning using successor- and predecessor-style representations in neural circuits.

Paper

DeepSeekMath: Pushing the Limits of Mathematical Reasoning in Open Language Models (Shao et al., 2024)

An example of reinforcement learning in large language models, using group-relative policy optimization to improve mathematical reasoning efficiently.
